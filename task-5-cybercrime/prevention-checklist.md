#  Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

As college students in India, we frequently use digital platforms for payments, communication, and learning. This makes us potential targets for cybercriminals. Following these practical and actionable safety tips can help protect your personal and financial information from online threats.

- Never share your OTP, passwords, bank details, or UPI PIN with anyone, even if they claim to be from a bank, customer care, or government authority.
- Always remember: entering your UPI PIN means you are **sending money**, not receiving it. No genuine refund or payment requires PIN entry to receive money.
- Do not approve unknown “collect requests” on apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm without verifying the sender’s identity.
- Avoid scanning QR codes sent by unknown people, as they may lead to unauthorized payments instead of receiving money.
- Double-check UPI IDs, mobile numbers, and payment details before sending money to avoid accidental or fraudulent transfers.
- Never click on suspicious links in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages claiming offers, prizes, refunds, or urgent account issues.
- Use strong and unique passwords for different accounts and enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or financial transactions, as such networks can be insecure and easily exploited.
- Regularly check your bank statements and transaction history to quickly identify any unauthorized activity.
- Install apps only from trusted sources like official app stores and keep your phone updated with the latest security patches.

##  Report Cybercrime Immediately

If you experience or suspect any cyber fraud, take action without delay:

-  National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in  
-  Helpline Number: 1930  

Prompt reporting can help stop further fraud and increase the chances of recovering lost money.
